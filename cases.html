Case studies

Four settings, three regimes

Each case is a distinct deployment class: hospital alert fatigue, ambulatory clinical decision support, chess tutoring, and C-MAPSS turbofan predictive maintenance. The numbers below are taken directly from the paper's benchmark tables and are anchored in published evidence or structurally specified environments.

Hospital pharmacy alert fatigue

Low drift

The hospital pharmacy benchmark is anchored in the fatigue estimate of Li & Piri (2025). Each clinical decision is accompanied by an optional medication-safety alert; the operator is a pharmacist or prescribing physician. The efficacy state tracks accumulated alert-fatigue.

With the published fatigue coefficient βd = 0.003394 (corresponding to βd = 0.05 on the paper's unified grid), dynamic signal design offers only a small improvement over a well-shaped myopic rule.

Takeaway. In this low-drift regime, the paper's null-model placebo reduces the RD gain below 0.3% — consistent with the safe-harbor logic of Proposition 3.3. A confidence-gated or accuracy-thresholded myopic rule is operationally adequate; the dynamic apparatus is only worth the investment if the deployment moves into a higher-drift regime.

Ambulatory clinical decision support

Boundary drift

The ambulatory CDS benchmark uses the encounter-level overload estimate of Ancker et al. (2017) to anchor a higher-drift calibration. Because the evidence pins down the average drift magnitude but not the low-efficacy responsiveness parameter, the paper sweeps this parameter across a band.

Across the band, RD gains over myopic range from 4.4% (high responsiveness) to 18.1% (low responsiveness). The SPD–RD wedge stays under 0.1 reward units everywhere.

C-MAPSS turbofan predictive maintenance

Engineering · structurally specified

C-MAPSS is NASA's Commercial Modular Aero-Propulsion System Simulation — a widely-used benchmark for turbofan degradation and remaining-useful-life prediction (Saxena et al. 2008). The paper uses it as a structurally specified environment where drift intensity is known and varied directly, isolating the efficacy-drift channel from broader deployment frictions.

The signal levels correspond to maintenance recommendations of increasing prescriptiveness: no action, inspection, corrective intervention, overhaul. The efficacy state reflects accumulated component wear and operating-regime coverage.

What this benchmark shows. With fully specified dynamics, the efficacy-drift channel is isolated cleanly. As drift rises, myopic signal allocation becomes materially suboptimal — not because the model is wrong, but because the dynamic channel dominates. Yet RD retains over 99% of SPD's value at every drift level, even when the two policies disagree on up to 12.8% of state-stage cells. The daylight is in policy shape, not policy value.

Chess tutoring and skill retention

Human–AI pedagogy

A chess tutor offers variable-intensity assistance at each puzzle or position: nothing, a gentle hint, a candidate-move list, or the full solution. Stronger assistance improves the immediate outcome of the puzzle but may erode long-run skill — the efficacy state is the trainee's accumulated positional understanding.

The paper validates this setting with a real-data benchmark drawn from online puzzle archives. Familiarity proxies — whether a position's opening has been seen before — serve as the planner-side efficacy signal. The expanded and deep-familiarity runs confirm the ordered-menu threshold structure and the value of dynamic design across sample sizes.

Reading across the cases

Each case is a data point on the paper's unified βd-grid. Placed side-by-side, they trace out a single monotone relationship: as efficacy drift rises, the gain from dynamic signal design rises from under 2% (hospital) through 4–10% (ambulatory high/mid) to over 20% (C-MAPSS high). RD tracks SPD within tenths of a percent throughout.

The practical implication: you don't need to commit to one design object across all deployments. You diagnose the drift regime and the specification regime of your setting, and the selection rule tells you which object to adopt.
