Signal policy lab

Policy simulator

A small Markov decision process with four signal levels (silent → hint → recommend → solve) and five efficacy classes. Compare three policies side-by-side: a myopic rule that picks the best signal now, an SPD policy that uses the planner's full cost model, and an RD policy that only knows relative intensity cost. All three are evaluated under true dynamics and the same budget.

How to read this

Low drift (βd < 0.1). The three curves overlap. Myopic signaling already captures nearly all of the value, and dynamic design adds little. A simple per-step rule is enough.

Material drift (βd ≈ 0.25 – 0.6). Myopic drifts below SPD and RD: greedy signaling depletes efficacy early and the system collects fewer late-horizon rewards. SPD and RD nearly coincide — confirming the paper's finding that RD retains 97–99.7% of SPD's value.

Add cost misspecification. Push the misspec slider above +30%. SPD now over-estimates state-dependent cost and becomes overcautious; it drops below RD. RD is invariant to this misspecification because it uses only intensity-ordered cost — the regularization story from Section 4.

Caveat. This is a pedagogical simulator — a small 5×20 tabular MDP with transparent primitives. Gains are directional but compressed compared to the paper's calibrated benchmarks, where high-drift C-MAPSS reaches 15–23% gains. The richer hospital pharmacy, ambulatory CDS, and C-MAPSS turbofan numbers are reported on the case studies page.
