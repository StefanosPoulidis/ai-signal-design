Diagnostic screener

Which design object fits your setting?

Five questions, one recommendation. The screener implements the paper's selection rule over design objects — myopic, RD, or SPD — conditioned on what your deployment can credibly identify and what cost object it can credibly defend.

How the screener works

The screener formalizes the paper's selection rule over design objects. The key insight is that the right design object — myopic, SPD, or RD — is not a tractability question. It is determined by what your deployment can credibly identify and what cost schedule it can credibly defend.

  • Myopic is appropriate when drift is absent or the horizon is too short for drift to accumulate. Under the safe-harbor condition of signal-independent efficacy (Proposition 3.3), confidence-gated and accuracy-thresholded rules are nearly optimal.
  • SPD is the oracle when the full constrained problem can be credibly specified: action logs, efficacy proxy, drift evidence, and a cardinal cost schedule B(x, ℓ) over all state–signal pairs.
  • RD is the constructive object when the planner can anchor aggregate tolerance and relative upgrades but not a full cardinal schedule. It retains 97–99.7% of SPD's value under correct specification and can outperform plug-in SPD under cost misspecification or order-breaking proxy noise.
  • Diagnostic-only applies when structural identification is impossible — you can still use the paper's screens (IDE decomposition, near-tie) as audit tools over whatever rule you run.
